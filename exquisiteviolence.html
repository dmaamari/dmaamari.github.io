Exquisite Violence: A Museum in the Making

BEIRUT, LEBANON | APEAL COMPETITION ENTRY

Advisor: Marwan Zouein

Old men playing cards, women hushing little children sleeping on mattresses, one next to the other in dark narrow corridors, listening to the radio for any news of explosives; a typical night in the life of my young father, Bassem. It is almost 5 AM now, the fire ceases, time to get up and go home to change and go to school. All is well as people go about their daily business; the women get to their house-keeping activities, the men go to work, and the children go to school walking. No vehicles are allowed, as Bassem crosses through barricaded streets. It is not until noon, that he hears the sound of a bomb as he is walking through the street. Shocked and confused, he tries to figure out where the sound is coming from to run away from it. He is left exposed and vulnerable as he makes his way back to the closest shelter. This made me think of the concept of exposed versus concealed space. The specificities of the site provided by APEAL NGO to design the Beirut Contemporary Art Museum located on the demarcation line, near the General National Security Building meant that there was much to be taken into consideration while designing the museum. Thus began the attempt to design a museum that is exposed within such stringent security factors . It all began with one question: “Can something be so exposed, that it becomes concealed?”


The museum as a flashy element of multiple fragments
A congregation of rooms where the content is exposed
These elevated entities intrude on the presumably stable containers of functions, allowing all users to become consumers of art. For instance, the following collage shows students sitting in the classroom. They are now able to leave it and get lost in the various parts of the museum, without having one directed path. A seemingly visible and accessible museum, is actually sometimes visible and inaccessible. However, for business users, for instance, a visible but inaccessible museum is necessary to avoid the distraction of the staff The museum experience is violent, since it strengthen the notion of the false stability, bringing back the experience of a regular day during the war, without being too literal
