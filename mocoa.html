Mocoa Model Resettlement Plan

MOCOA AND BOGOTA, COLOMBIA

Partners: MIT Environmental Solutions Initiative and a range of Colombian stakeholders (governmental entities, educational institutions, civil rights groups, private businesses, and the indigenous Amazonian communities)
Advisors: Professor John Fernandez
Team: Diego Castillo, Jacob Kohn, and Daniella Maamari


Mocoa Model Resettlement Plan is a disaster risk management project in Mocoa, Colombia. My team traveled to Colombia to learn about the deadly mudslides that have impacted the Amazonian city. We met with individuals from the community, experts in biodiversity and urban planning, indigenous leaders, government officials and conducted workshops to determine impactful projects we can develop with, and for, the community. My team developed a geospatial model for land occupation prioritization and density analysis that was adopted by the environmental planning authority of the Colombian Amazon. This actionable, site-specific, post disaster resettlement plan will be used to resettle the populations affected by the multiple mudslides. It was an eye-opening experience that showed me the importance of identifying challenges and designing both “preventive” and “curative” solutions to social, economic or natural shock.

Geospatial Framework and Analysis


To compare and suggest the best possible locations for land occupation for resettlement and growth, we organized our analysis in the following broad steps: -Identify current urbanization requirements for Mocoa
-Map the environmental restrictions and potential land for development
-Assign scores to municipal area and limit scope of analysis
-Aggregate polygons and scores to populated towns
-Rank the populated areas based on their average scores
-Compare the availability with the population and housing requirements

Density Analysis

Upon selecting the most suitable site and highest ranking between our list and that of the environmental planning authority, we proceeded to determine reasonable densities for the new resettlement plan. We conducted site analysis of existing densities, land area, population, lot size, house size, roads, setbacks, volumes, and topography and supplemented it with case studies of resettlement plans in the vicinity. We visualized three possible densities: low, medium, and high densities. Our analysis allowed us to recommend a density based on informed analysis of the need for resettlement, the urban growth patterns of peri-urban areas around Mocoa, and density visualization. We provided a site and density that accommodates all the needed new housing, for the next 20 years, with a density that allocates sufficient surface area for agriculture.
