Zayouna International School

BAGHDAD, IRAQ (UNDER CONSTRUCTION)


Team: Ahmad Beydoun, Sandra Hajj, Ghida Khayat, Daniella Maamari,and Hadi Nasreldine

Concept

The research phase of this project included a brief glance at the architectural wealth and historical development of the city of Baghdad throughout history. The theme of the “wall” once held connotations of power, fortification, and novel urban planning methods such as the “Round City of Baghdad”. Unfortunately, it now suffers from its fate as an extensively walled war-torn Baghdad.

Design Process

Our challenge in the concept development phase involved bypassing the security restrictions that impose high fences on all developments in the region, by intensifying it and incorporating it within the built structure in an innovative manner.

To do so, we positioned the built area on the peripheries of the site, thickened the peripheral walls, incorporated the school’s four main departments and facilities within them, and minimized apertures on the ground floor level.

In our school, the wall as strategy, structure and program performs an inclusive and inviting role that engages with its context and users. The neighboring mosque’s porch crosses the school’s site limit, but by responding to the protests of the neighbors, we managed to incorporate a plug-in that serves as an extension to the mosque and marketplace during Friday prayers times, and an entrance and public space to the school. The thick but porous wall thus breaks at certain moments releasing functions related to the arts, sciences, culture, and public functions catering to the existing context.
