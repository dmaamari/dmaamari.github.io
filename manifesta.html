Manifesta 13 Urban Study: "Le Grand Puzzle"

MARSEILLE, FRANCE

Book Chapter Division

Role: Assistant Designer at MVRDV (Design | Research | Writing)

The French city of Marseille is set to host Manifesta 13, the European Biennial of Contemporary Art in autumn 2020. In preparation for the event, Manifesta 13 collaborated with MVRDV and its think-tank The Why Factor (T?F) in preparing the pre-biennial research on the city of Marseille. The interdisciplinary urban study produced by MVRDV and T?F allowed the exhibition management to understand the needs of the city and set up a permanent pavilion in Marseille, France. Since Manifesta is a nomadic art biennial, hosted in a new “second” European city every time, this research foregrounds the city of Marseille itself and allows for a site-specific strategy for the biennial and the spaces it occupies. As an assistant designer, I designed, researched, and wrotes parts of the study, “Le Grand Puzzle”. I contributed written chapters to the study, which encompassed a range of topics like sustainability, safety, transportation networks, natural resources, economy, history of development, to nam a few. I designed maps and infographics, researched and analyzed spatial and qualitative data on the city of Marseille. “Le Grand Puzzle” will be published as a book in September 2020.
