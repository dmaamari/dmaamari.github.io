XXth Chilean Biennial:"Byblos Blue"

VALPARAISO, CHILE

Jobson, Christopher. "The Cyanometer Is a 225-Year-Old Tool for Measuring the Blueness of the Sky". Colossal(blog).May 9, 2014.

"Byblos Blue" Installation at the XXth Chilean Architecture and Urbanism Biennial: "Dailogos Impostergables"

Role: Assistant Designer & Researcher at Hashim Sarkis Studios

The concept of the cyanometer, a ring measuring the different shades of blue in the sky, was developed by Alexander for Humboldt. He placed the ring against the sky, as a means to describe the blueness of every new geography and landscape. The “Byblos Blue” installation at the XXth Architecture and Urbanism Biennial “Dialogos Impostergables”, presented architectural models of the Byblos Town Hall (designed by Hashim Sarkis Studios) and two other proposals for public spaces in the historic city center of Byblos, Lebanon. These models were placed on adjustable supports that allowed visitors to view the different proposals against a range of different shades of blue sky. The installation hinted at the notion of measurable, discernable factors that guide the design of specific site interventions through a simple, powerful, and informative installation on the history of the cyanometer and the development of historic Byblos.
My role as assistant designer and researcher encompassed preparing the architectural models of the different urban development projects. In October 2017, I led the construction team in setting up the installation at the Parque Cultural de Valparaiso, Chile.
